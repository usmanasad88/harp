# Indus RAS Expo 2026 — Exhibitors Directory

Approved exhibitors at Indus RAS Expo 2026 (22–23 July 2026, Pakistan China Friendship Centre, Islamabad) include defence manufacturers, robotics and drone companies, AI and deep-tech firms, universities, schools and EdTech organizations. Each exhibitor below is listed with its sector and a short profile.

> Source: https://indusrasexpo.gov.pk/exhibitors (fetched 2026-07-19)

### Aerosolutions Pvt Ltd — aviation and flight simulators

Aerosolutions (Private) Limited is a trusted aviation solutions provider with a legacy dating back to 1969, supporting fixed-wing and rotary-wing aircraft through engineering, maintenance, training and technical services. It specializes in the design, development and lifecycle support of advanced flight simulators, including full-motion, fixed-base, VR and XR simulators for pilot training, emergency procedures, instrument flying, mission rehearsal and crew coordination, each customizable to aircraft type and cockpit configuration. It also offers aircraft maintenance, repair and overhaul, avionics integration, training, consultancy, and spare parts support.

### AXI Technologies — robotics, AI and cybersecurity

AXI Technologies, part of the AXI Systems ecosystem, is a Pakistan-based innovation company delivering solutions in artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, software development, cloud services, robotics, drones, embedded systems, digital forensics and enterprise automation, for government, defence, education, enterprise and commercial sectors. Capabilities include AI chatbots, predictive analytics, computer vision, secure communication platforms, cybersecurity audits and penetration testing, autonomous platforms, sensor integration and custom engineering. AXI also supports digital skills, robotics education and professional certification.

### Beaconhouse International College — education

Beaconhouse International College offers a learning journey aimed at shaping future industry leaders, with approachable faculty, diverse peers, and support services including EPCAD for employability and placements. Its facilities and teaching methods focus on equipping students with industry-relevant skills, academic excellence and career readiness.

### Cavalier Group — defence manufacturing and UAV surveillance

Cavalier Group is a multidomain defence manufacturer delivering indigenous mission-critical solutions for the Defence Forces of Pakistan and allied countries. As a technology integrator and original equipment manufacturer in land, air, IT and cyber domains, its activities span research, design, development, manufacturing and integration of combat vehicles, personal weapons, ballistic protection, tactical gear, UAV drones, system automation and cybersecurity.

### Comcept — AI, edge computing and fire control systems

Comcept, founded in 1992 in Islamabad, delivers indigenous products and services in the telecom, energy and defence sectors, with a 30-year record on mission-critical systems. Recent original design work covers fire control systems (FCS) with full sensory inter-working and auto-tracking, including localization of the VT-4 tank's FCS. Comcept envisions next-generation land weapon systems including an advanced infantry mortar system and a remote weapon station.

### ConnecTech — robots, AI and autonomous solutions

ConnecTech, based at the National Aerospace Science and Technology Park (NASTP) in Rawalpindi, is a hub for technological innovation and industrial co-creation, connecting industries with a network of over 1,500 scientists, engineers and research institutes to deliver market-ready technology. It works through industrial co-creation and joint R&D, technology transfer, and process engineering / toll manufacturing, across four verticals: industrial automation, smart agriculture and biotechnology, healthcare and life sciences, and aerospace, defence tech and space applications.

### CubeX LMS — AI, robotics and unmanned ground vehicles

CubeX LMS is an AI and robotics company developing adaptable solutions for education, defence and industry. At RAS Expo it presents Cu-bit 4.0 Pro, a compact unmanned ground vehicle (UGV) used in education to demonstrate autonomous navigation, obstacle avoidance, sensing and robotic manipulation. The modular platform can be expanded with cameras, communication modules, environmental sensors or task-specific payloads, with applications in indoor material transport, remote inspection of hazardous areas, and autonomous patrol support.

### Department of Mechatronics Engineering, College of EME, NUST — education and robotics research

The Department of Mechatronics Engineering at the College of Electrical and Mechanical Engineering (CEME), National University of Sciences and Technology (NUST), is a leading centre for education and research in robotics, automation, artificial intelligence and intelligent systems. Research spans humanoid robots, autonomous ground and underwater vehicles, robotic manipulators, computer vision, SLAM, machine learning, reinforcement learning, intelligent control and human-robot interaction. The department organizes the National Engineering Robotics Contest (NERC). At Indus RAS Expo it showcases intelligent robotic platforms, autonomous navigation, AI-powered perception and embedded intelligence.

### Directorate General Research & Development Establishment (DGRDE) — defence R&D and UAV surveillance

RDE serves the Tri-Services, defence production establishments, law enforcement agencies, civil government organizations and friendly foreign countries, through partnerships with public and private industry, academia and R&D organizations. It promotes technology diffusion across the defence sector, develops a skilled workforce for advanced technologies, and expands local development and production capabilities toward self-reliance and technological independence.

### Headstart School — education, STEM and Pakistan Flying Labs

Established in 1991, Headstart School is one of Pakistan's leading K–12 institutions, offering Cambridge and International Baccalaureate (IB) programmes across campuses in Islamabad, Rawalpindi and Lahore with over 3,000 students. A pioneer in STEM education, robotics, AI, programming, engineering design and drone technology, it runs hands-on Design Labs and the Robofort Centre for Engineering and Robotics. Headstart hosts Pakistan Flying Labs, the country's only officially licensed member of the global Flying Labs Network by WeRobotics.

### Heavy Industries Taxila (HIT) — defence production and unmanned aerial systems

Heavy Industries Taxila is Pakistan's premier defence production organization under the Ministry of Defence Production. Established in 1971, HIT specializes in the design, manufacture, rebuild, overhaul and upgrade of armoured fighting vehicles, tanks, armoured personnel carriers, artillery systems, security vehicles, and unmanned aerial systems (UAS). It serves the Pakistan Armed Forces and law enforcement, and promotes exports and international collaboration.

### Horizon Tech Services — cybersecurity, AI and drones

Horizon Tech Services is a Pakistani technology company working in information assurance, cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, drone technologies and secure communication solutions. It helps public- and private-sector organizations protect sensitive information, strengthen digital infrastructure, and enhance operational capabilities through certified products and professional expertise.

### Ignite National Technology Fund — technology and innovation

Ignite is a public-sector organization under the Ministry of Information Technology and Telecommunication (MoITT), Government of Pakistan. Its mission is to accelerate Pakistan's transformation into a knowledge-based economy by fostering innovation, entrepreneurship, research commercialization, digital skills and emerging technologies, through National Incubation Centers (NICs), technology innovation grants, the Pakistan Startup Fund, DigiSkills.pk, and programs in AI, cybersecurity, cloud computing, gaming and other Fourth Industrial Revolution technologies. Ignite has supported thousands of startups and enabled millions of digital skills trainings.

### Trovix Global Pvt Ltd — research commercialization for the Institute of Space Technology

Trovix Global (TG) is the strategic commercial interface for the Institute of Space Technology (IST), bridging academic research and industry. It commercializes IST's R&D capabilities, facilitating technology transfer, technical consultancy and collaborative innovation for public and private sectors, leveraging IST's multidisciplinary expertise in space technology, engineering and advanced sciences.

### LearnOBots — STEAM education technology

LearnOBots, established in 2014, is a leading EdTech company transforming education through STEAM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts and Mathematics), AI, robotics and emerging technologies. Its ecosystem includes the LearnOSTEAM digital learning platform, STEAM Labs, makerspaces, educational hardware kits, teacher training and innovation challenges, spanning AI, robotics, coding, IoT, electronics, 3D design and printing, drones, cybersecurity and data science.

### Maritime Technical & Support Services (MTSS) — autonomous maritime systems

MTSS provides maritime technology, logistics, engineering and project management services to the Pakistan Navy and other national and international organizations, operating within a corporate framework compliant with Government of Pakistan regulations, with a focus on reliable, efficient and cost-effective solutions.

### MatEnTx Pvt Ltd — piezoelectric sensors, MEMS and soft robotics

MatEnTx is a NUST spin-off that translates academic research into market-ready piezoelectric, MEMS and soft-robotics technologies. Core expertise includes piezoelectric sensors and actuators (ultrasonic actuators, hydrophones, precision transducers), MEMS devices for compact low-power sensing, compliant soft-robotics sensors, and material testing and characterization. It delivers actuators, sensors and energy-harvesting modules for robotics, industrial automation, underwater vehicles and autonomous systems, leveraging the Robot Maker Lab and the Micro and Nano Robotics Technologies Lab at NUST CEME.

### Mediatiz Foundation — EdTech and AI for youth

Mediatiz Foundation is a Pakistan-based nonprofit empowering youth through AI, robotics, autonomous systems and emerging technologies. It develops AI-powered solutions, government-approved bilingual curricula, immersive learning platforms, robotics training, AR/VR experiences and educational technologies. Within a year it reached over 1 million learners, trained 21,000+ educators, collaborated with 5,000+ schools, and launched the Khudi App with 90,000+ downloads. It is a member of the UNESCO Global Media and Information Literacy Alliance.

### Musanna Log & Tech Services Pvt Ltd — engineering, simulators and robotics

MUSANNA Log & Tech Svc (MLT) is a holding company delivering specialized solutions across engineering, simulators, robotics and electric mobility. Its divisions include Aero Solution (aviation simulators and global tech support) and Musanna Tech (EV systems, industrial automation, robotics, UAVs, BLDC motors).

### Musanna Tech Pvt Ltd — manufacturing, AI, hardware and software

Musanna Technical (Pvt) Ltd, based in Rawalpindi, is a multidisciplinary engineering and manufacturing company serving defence, industrial, infrastructure and mobility sectors, drawing expertise from the Electrical and Mechanical Engineers (EME) Corps. Established in September 2024 as the front office of the Corps of EME, its core divisions cover maintenance, repair and overhaul; indigenous manufacturing; design and simulation; HR and consultancy; and AI and software.

### NaqCode Tech Pvt Ltd — computational design for aerial and ground platforms

NaqCoDE is a computational design company specializing in the design, analysis and optimization of aerial and ground platforms, including combat UAVs. Its multidisciplinary team applies computational fluid dynamics (CFD), finite element analysis (FEA) and multidisciplinary optimization to deliver optimized designs, reducing the need for expensive experimental testing.

### National Radio & Telecommunication Corporation (NRTC) — defence electronics, UGVs, UAVs and counter-drone systems

NRTC is Pakistan's premier technology enterprise for the design, development, manufacturing, integration and lifecycle support of advanced defence, communication and IT solutions. As a trusted OEM it delivers tactical communications, electronic warfare, anti-drone (C-UAS) systems, robotics, unmanned systems (UGVs, UAVs, robodogs, logistics robots), cybersecurity, C6ISR, AI, IoT and smart-city technologies, supporting projects from concept to production, deployment, training and after-sales service.

### National University of Sciences and Technology (NUST), College of EME — RoboWars host

The College of Electrical and Mechanical Engineering (CEME) is a prime hub of engineering education in Pakistan and heads the RoboWars competition at Indus RAS Expo. The college traces its history to the EME School established at Quetta in 1957, elevated to college status by 1969, and started its own BSc engineering programme in 1981.

### NCRA Robot Maker Lab — robot prototyping and manufacturing

The Robot Maker Lab (RML) is a flagship research, prototyping and manufacturing facility of the National Centre of Robotics and Automation (NCRA), located at the NUST College of Electrical and Mechanical Engineering, Rawalpindi. NCRA is a national consortium of 11 specialized laboratories across 13 universities headquartered at NUST CEME. RML converts robotic concepts, engineering designs and research outcomes into functional prototypes, providing rapid prototyping, machining, fabrication and system-integration support for indigenous robotics.

### NCRA Swarm Robotics Lab, UET Taxila — intelligent robotics and Edge AI

The Swarm Robotics Lab (SRL) at UET Taxila, established under the National Centre of Robotics and Automation (NCRA), advances intelligent robotics, AI, autonomous systems and human-centred automation. At the expo SRL showcases indigenous innovations including AI-powered educational robots, autonomous mobile robots, robotic manipulators, drone technologies, machine vision systems and Edge-AI solutions, with demonstrations of autonomous navigation, computer vision, multi-robot coordination and STEM education platforms.

### Nexa Robots Pvt Ltd — smart service robots

Nexa Robots is a pioneering AI robotics company in Pakistan providing advanced autonomous service robots for healthcare, hospitality, education, retail, corporate, commercial and industrial environments. Beyond robot deployment it offers system integration, software customization, AI development, installation, training and after-sales support, collaborating with global robotics manufacturers while developing localized AI solutions including multilingual capabilities.

### NRG Tech Pvt Ltd — brain-controlled robotics and neurotechnology

NRG Tech is a deep-tech company focused on neurotechnology, rehabilitation engineering, assistive robotics, AI, biomedical engineering and STEM education. It is a spin-off of the Neuroimaging Research Group (NRG) at the Department of Mechatronics Engineering, Air University, in collaboration with the National Centre of Robotics and Automation (NCRA). Its research roots cover brain-machine interfaces (BMI), brain-robot interfaces (BRI), bio-robotics, pattern recognition, neuroimaging, neuroscience and neurolinguistics.

### Sarwar Engineering House — precision drone parts and defence manufacturing

Sarwar Engineering House has broad experience manufacturing assemblies and parts for aerospace, weapons and defence industries, specializing in high-precision drone parts and assemblies. Its focus is indigenization of high-tech engineering parts, aiming at import substitution and export of world-class, cost-effective engineering products to OEMs globally.

### Supreme Technocrafts Aviation Services — deep-tech and aerospace

Supreme Technocrafts is a deep-tech and aerospace company dealing with space and AI technologies, maintenance repair and overhaul (MRO), technical consultancy, and domestic manufacturing of machinery.

### Switzern / Stratheon — industrial robotics and automation

Switzern Group, including Stratheon and Switzern Technik, delivers integrated industrial automation, precision manufacturing and turnkey engineering. Building on the legacy of Ashraf Welding (1980s), the group specializes in robotic welding; laser, plasma and waterjet cutting; pick-and-place automation; and advanced motion systems, with precision machining of HSLA, aluminium and titanium alloys. Since 2015 Switzern has executed automation, robotics and manufacturing projects for Pakistan's defence organizations and major automotive OEMs.

### Tech Valley — defence engineering solutions

Tech Valley is a specialized engineering and technology solutions provider enhancing the operational readiness, maintenance capability and lifecycle management of defence equipment through indigenous innovation — developing inspection, diagnostics, maintenance and automation systems that improve the efficiency, safety and reliability of military platforms.

### UAS Global (UASG) — unmanned aerial systems and counter-drone solutions

UAS Global (Pvt) Ltd, established in 2016, is one of Pakistan's leading providers of unmanned aerial systems (UAS), counter-unmanned aerial systems (C-UAS) and aerial intelligence. It delivers UAV technologies, aerial surveying, mapping, inspection services and specialized defence solutions to government, military, law enforcement and commercial sectors, including system integration, consultancy, pilot training, maintenance and mission planning.

### Voyager Electronics — unmanned systems and combat UAVs

Voyager Electronics is a Pakistan-based technology company specializing in R&D, manufacturing, integration and lifecycle support of advanced unmanned systems and mission-critical electronics. Its portfolio includes ISR platforms, FPV drones, interceptor drones, logistics drones, heavy-lift UAVs and customized mission-specific solutions, plus complete mission ecosystems with ground control stations (GCS), communication links, telemetry, payload integration and power management.

### vResolve — AI, embedded systems and UAV engineering

vResolve is a multidisciplinary technology and engineering company developing products that combine AI, embedded systems, electronics, robotics, autonomous platforms, IoT, cloud and aerospace engineering. Its experience includes Edge AI, autonomous vehicles, industrial monitoring, medical-device software, custom electronics, surveillance, predictive analytics, aircraft simulation, agricultural UAV localization, swarm UAV systems and interceptor UAV design — with more than 35,000 engineering hours, 50 completed projects and 10 market-ready products.

### ZATNav Pvt Ltd — GNSS navigation, simulators and defence technology

ZATNav is a Pakistan-based deep-technology company at the intersection of GNSS navigation, artificial intelligence, aerospace simulation and defence technology. Headquartered at Multi Gardens B-17, Islamabad, it designs and delivers mission-critical systems and training simulators for aerospace, defence and enterprise clients.
