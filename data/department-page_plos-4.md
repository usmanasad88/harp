# Mechatronics programme learning outcomes (PLOs) — NUST CEME

The Mechatronics Engineering programme at NUST College of EME aims to instill twelve attributes in its graduates:

- PLO 1, Engineering Knowledge — apply mathematics, science and engineering fundamentals to complex engineering problems.
- PLO 2, Problem Analysis — identify, formulate, research and analyze complex engineering problems using first principles.
- PLO 3, Design and Development of Solutions — design systems, components or processes meeting specified needs with consideration for public health, safety, culture, society and environment.
- PLO 4, Investigation — investigate complex problems methodically: literature survey, experiment design, data analysis and synthesis of valid conclusions.
- PLO 5, Modern Tool Usage — create, select and apply appropriate techniques and modern engineering and IT tools, understanding their limitations.
- PLO 6, The Engineer and Society — assess societal, health, safety, legal and cultural issues relevant to professional engineering practice.
- PLO 7, Environment and Sustainability — understand the societal and environmental impact of engineering solutions and the need for sustainable development.
- PLO 8, Ethics — apply ethical principles and professional responsibility.
- PLO 9, Individual and Teamwork — work effectively alone or in multidisciplinary teams.
- PLO 10, Communication — communicate effectively, orally and in writing, on complex engineering activities.
- PLO 11, Project Management — apply management skills and engineering principles to manage projects in multidisciplinary environments.
- PLO 12, Lifelong Learning — recognize the importance of, and pursue, lifelong learning amid innovation and technological change.
