# Department of Mechatronics Engineering — NUST College of EME

Mechatronics combines mechanical, computer, electronics and information sciences to design intelligent systems, balancing mechanical structure with advanced control. The College of Electrical and Mechanical Engineering introduced Mechatronics in 1998 to meet the demands of industries transitioning from traditional mechanical systems to fully automated, computer-controlled processes. Graduates contribute to automation, defence, R&D and advanced industrial systems, excelling in control engineering, robotics, AI, machine vision and beyond.

## Mechatronics Head of Department message — Dr Hamid Jabbar

As Head of Mechatronics Engineering, Dr Hamid Jabbar is committed to innovation and excellence in academics, research and commercialization, adopting the Triple Helix Model to enhance student skills and employability through industry and government funding. Focus areas include developing industrial machines with the Triple Diamond design process, establishing new labs, and delivering funded projects through final-year design projects, MS and PhD theses, and NUST spinoff companies. The department integrates university, industry and government efforts through the National Centre for Robotics and Automation (NCRA), continues hosting the National Engineering Robotics Contest (NERC) and the IEEE-sponsored International Conference on Robotics and Automation in Industry (ICRAI), and is enhancing its industry-funded PLC lab and establishing a new Industrial AI and IoT research lab.

## Mechatronics department research groups

The department's research groups: Industrial AI and IoT (IAI) Research Group, Advanced Manufacturing and Automation Group, Micro and Nano Robotics Group, Agrobotics Research Group, Unmanned Systems Group, and Bio Robotics Group.

## Mechatronics department vision and mission

To impart high-quality education in mechatronics engineering through excellence in teaching, research and entrepreneurship.
