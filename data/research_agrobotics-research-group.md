# Agrobotics Research Group — Mechatronics Engineering, NUST CEME

The Agrobotics Research Group of the Department of Mechatronics Engineering, NUST CEME, focuses on automation and robotics for the agricultural sector, integrating advanced sensing, machine vision, AI-driven decision-making, precision mechatronic systems and advanced manufacturing to make farming more efficient. It has two primary streams — autonomous field robotics and smart agro-processing systems — and uses the shared "Agri-domain" facility of the Robot Design and Development Lab, NCRA. Key themes: autonomous navigation for agricultural robots, AI-driven crop monitoring and disease detection, robotic harvesting and sorting, automated irrigation and resource management, UAVs for precision agriculture, and sustainable agro-mechatronics.

## Agrobotics Research Group — featured projects, funding and PhDs

Featured projects: Autonomous Multipurpose Agribot for precision farming, AI-enabled robotic system for smart weeding, seeding and harvesting, machine-learning-based fruit maturity detection, UAV-based non-destructive fruit quality inspection, autonomous drone-based crop monitoring and health assessment, and autonomous weed removal robots.

Funding: "Design and Development of Multipurpose Agribot for Smart Farming" (PKR 3.2 million).

PhD dissertations: Ayesha Zeb, "Development of Decision Support System for Non-Destructive Fruit Quality Estimation" (graduated December 2022); Syed Imran Moazzam Shah, "Efficient Classification of Weeds in Crops for Autonomous Spray Application" (graduated January 2023).
