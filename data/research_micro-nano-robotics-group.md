# Micro and Nano Robotics Group — Mechatronics Engineering, NUST CEME

The Micro and Nano Robotics Group of the Department of Mechatronics Engineering, NUST CEME, runs the Micro and Nano Robotics Technologies Lab (MNRTL), equipped for the design, analysis and testing of MEMS devices and the synthesis, characterization and testing of piezoelectric material-based sensors for applications including biomedical devices. Members: Dr Hamid Jabbar (Associate Professor — Piezo-MEMS), Dr Muhammad Osama Ali (Assistant Professor — MEMS), Dr Hassan Elahi (Associate Professor — Piezo-MEMS), and Dr Danish Hussain (Associate Professor — nano robotics).

## Micro and Nano Robotics Group — funding

ICRG funding: "Development of Nanomaterials-based Tactile Sensors for Tele-Manipulation in Robotic Surgery" — joint funding of PKR 96 million from HEC (43.967 M) and the British Council (52.481 M).

## Micro and Nano Robotics Lab — design, analysis and test facilities

Software: MEMS+, Coventorware, MATLAB, COMSOL, ANSYS. Specialized test equipment: C-6 probe station, optical microscope, dynamic signal analyzer, data acquisition cards, LCR meter, rolling ball mill, heat press, vacuum drying oven, high-voltage DC power supply, plus power supplies, digital multimeters, oscilloscopes and digital force gauges (300 mN to 50 N).
