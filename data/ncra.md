# NCRA — National Centre of Robotics and Automation

The National Centre of Robotics and Automation (NCRA), founded in 2018, is a PSDP-funded project under the Higher Education Commission (HEC). It is a consortium of 11 labs established at 13 universities across Pakistan, with its central headquarters at the NUST College of E&ME, Rawalpindi. The centre serves as Pakistan's leading technological hub for robotics and automation. Website: https://ncra.org.pk

> Source: https://ceme.nust.edu.pk/ncra (fetched 2026-07-19)

## NCRA vision and mission

Vision: enabling highly skilled experts to build an ecosystem focused on indigenous technology development using robotics and automation, making Pakistan one of the technologically advanced nations of the world. Mission: facilitate a consortium of experts across the country to promote research and development in robotics and automation — promoting the knowledge economy, achieving technology indigenization, developing highly skilled human resources, and fulfilling the SDGs.

### NCRA Robot Maker Lab — NUST College of E&ME

Equipped with state-of-the-art rapid prototyping, machining and fabrication facilities for robot parts. It is a shared facility among all NCRA labs, acting as a centralized robot manufacturing facility supporting NCRA labs, startups, academia and industry.

### NCRA Robot Design and Development Lab — NUST College of E&ME

A fusion centre where real-world problems are analyzed by a multidisciplinary team and led through to commercialization. It also develops human resources and provides a platform where industry, startups and academia collaborate.

### NCRA Human Centered Robotics Lab — UET Lahore and UCP Lahore

A joint lab of UET and UCP Lahore that indigenously designs integrated robotic systems based on smart sensing and actuation to interact with and learn from humans. Application domains: collaborative robots, exoskeletons, active prostheses and active orthoses.

### NCRA Industrial Monitoring and Automation Lab — ITU Lahore

Solves commercial and public-sector industrial problems: real-time health monitoring of induction motors (the workhorse of industry), analysis of power consumption, design improvement recommendations, and industrial energy audits.

### NCRA MEMS Sensor Design and Testing Lab — Air University

Develops world-class MEMS expertise in design and testing, supporting academic institutions, strategic organizations and robotics research groups. Work covers MEMS physical sensors, microrobotic actuators, RF-MEMS and micro-scale energy harvesters.

### NCRA Agricultural Robotics Lab — LUMS

A national precision-agriculture facility focused on agricultural robotics and automation, helping Pakistani farmers improve production through efficient water use, better crop monitoring, yield prediction, and reduced harmful agricultural inputs.

### NCRA UAV Dependability Lab — FAST NUCES

Focuses on automated UAV testing: software-in-the-loop (SiL), hardware-in-the-loop (HiL), and adapting these technologies to civil domains, applicable to both military and civil unmanned aircraft systems. Collaborates with the Aviation Design Institute at Pakistan Aeronautical Complex, Kamra.

### NCRA Swarm Robotics Lab — UET Taxila

Develops scalable, application-agnostic multi-agent robotic solutions using swarm intelligence algorithms. Core areas: swarm intelligence, AI and computer vision, and decentralized communication.

### NCRA Haptics, Human-Robotics and Condition Monitoring Lab — NED Karachi and MUET

A joint lab: NED University handles haptic and tele-robotic interfaces for disaster management and medical applications plus STEM-based education and assistive robots; Mehran University (MUET) develops predictive-maintenance condition monitoring for transport infrastructure (railways and roads).

### NCRA Control Automotive and Robotics Lab — BUITEMS and MUST

A joint lab solving pressing problems using robotics and AI. BUITEMS works on evolutionary robotics, deep learning, search and rescue robotics, social robotics, textile-based robotic sensors and data mining; MUST develops a level-4 autonomous vehicle and an autonomous industrial loader for freight transport.

### NCRA Advanced Robotics and Automation Lab — UET Peshawar

Works on prosthesis and orthosis, and agricultural machinery and robotics, developing indigenous solutions for prosthetic and orthotic devices and intelligent agricultural machinery.

## NCRA Research Fund

The NCRA research fund supports research in robotics and automation, fostering the transition from research to integration and business. From 40 proposals received nationwide (call published 31 May 2020), 9 were shortlisted through a three-stage evaluation, including: a CNC kitchen gardening robot (Univ. of Agriculture Faisalabad), an Industry 4.0 IIoT test-bed (LUMS), a universal gun barrel cleaning mechanism (NUST), a BCI-based exoskeleton for elderly and disabled people (Air University), RoboDoc social robot for healthcare (NED), a full flight motion platform (PAF-KIET), piezoelectric devices (Dr Hamid Jabbar, NUST), a shape-memory-polymer soft actuator (Air University), and textile warehouse automation with Industry 4.0 (FAST NUCES and NUST).

## NCRA Robotics and Automation Startup Challenge

NCRA annually hosts the Robotics and Automation Startup Challenge to encourage young startups, professionals and graduating students to develop ideas that help local industry reach Industry 4.0 standards, solve societal problems, and create jobs. Themes: indigenous product development for automation of local industry, and indigenous products solving societal problems with robotics and automation. Challenges were held in December 2020 and December 2021, with the third edition's pitching rounds in July 2022.

## ICRAI — International Conference on Robotics and Automation in Industry

The NCRA secretariat organized the 3rd and 4th IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation in Industry (ICRAI) in October 2019 and October 2021, attracting global research publications and top keynote speakers, with a 30% paper acceptance rate, plus trainings, workshops, and the Lab2Market product showcase. Keynote speakers included Prof Jun Ho Oh (South Korea), Prof Karsten Berns (Germany), Prof Peter Corke (Australia) in 2019, and Prof Manoj Karkee (USA), Prof William Melek (Canada), Dr Carl Diver (Manchester Metropolitan University, UK) in 2021.
