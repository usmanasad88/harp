9\. **<span class="underline">Project Details:</span>**

1.  Introduction

> Intelligent robots provide us with a chance to deploy Artificial Intelligence in our daily life especially as facilitators. Such robots are possible candidates as task facilitators in various daily life activities including cleaning tasks, delivery robots, receptionist bots etc. Such robots belong to the category of “socially interactive robots (SIR)”.
> 
> A "roboceptionist" was employed in Carnegie Mellon University, and it featured a conversational agent called Valerie displayed on a screen and interacting with visitors through a dialogue system . Another such robot called AKSA was deployed at NAIST Japan in 2003. ASKA is a prototype research-purpose oriented robot using visual recognition, speech recognition, voice response, web retrieval, PDP display, mechatronics and internet communication. ASKA can recognize a user’s question utterance for the university related matters, and answer by synthesized voice with its hand gesture and head movement. “Wakamaru” is a humanoid robot known with its distinct yellow shell color. It was introduced in 2005 by Mitsubishi as a receptionist and domestic assistant. Some more recent SARs include iCub (2014) and DEVI (2019). iCub robot is a humanoid receptionist robot which can guide the visitors towards the location of various places in university premises with the help of a map.

2.  Summary of research Methodology

> The system harnesses **Edge Computing** using the **NVIDIA Jetson Nano Development Kit** as the primary platform to execute complex AI tasks directly at the source of data generation. This approach minimizes latency and bandwidth usage. **ROS2** is employed for efficient data exchange and synchronization among sensors and modules. The system comprises five main modules, each with specialized submodules:

1.  **Sensing Module**  
    Acquires data from the external environment using various sensors: Sensors include voice, video, and proximity detectors.

2.  **Perception Module**

> The **Perception Module** is a critical component that processes and interprets data gathered by the Sensing Module to provide meaningful insights about the environment and user interactions. It utilizes advanced algorithms to extract and analyze information from multiple data streams, enabling the robot to understand and respond effectively to its surroundings. This module consists of the following key submodules:
> 
> **Facial Recognition**:  
> The Facial Recognition submodule processes facial data captured by the video sensor to recognize and identify human users by **their gender and age** as well. By leveraging facial analysis algorithms, it can differentiate between individuals, providing personalized interactions. This capability ensures that the robot can maintain continuity in its interactions and tailor its responses to specific users.
> 
> **Speech Recognition**:  
> The Speech Recognition submodule processes audio data from the robot's voice sensors to transcribe spoken words into text. Using advanced natural language processing algorithms, it deciphers users' speech patterns, accents, and intonations to ensure accurate transcription. This enables the robot to understand verbal commands, engage in meaningful dialogue, and interpret users' needs effectively.
> 
> Together, these submodules empower the Perception Module to create a detailed understanding of the environment and user interactions, forming the foundation for the system's intelligent decision-making and responsiveness.

3.  **Cognition Module**  
    Generates appropriate responses and actions based on user interactions:

> **Natural Language Processing**: Manages dialogue and generates text-based communication using templates and strategies.
> 
> **Recommender System**: Provides relevant suggestions by retrieving, filtering, ranking, and recommending information based on criteria like relevance or popularity.

4.  **User Interface Module**  
    Facilitates interaction through:

> A tablet screen on the robot’s chest for text/graphics display and touchscreen input.
> 
> Audio outputs via speakers on the robot's head.

3.  Objectives of the project

> **To develop HARP, a humanoid assistive robot with robust perception, seamless human–robot interaction, and safe autonomous mobility**, capable of reliable operation in real-world service environments.
> 
> **To enhance operational efficiency and customer satisfaction** by delivering consistent, personalized, and uninterrupted services through AI-driven learning and decision-making.
> 
> **To integrate advanced technologies including natural language processing, computer vision, and machine learning** to enable a scalable and cost-effective service solution.

4.  Capability achieved

> **A fully functional prototype of HARP** was developed, demonstrating robust perception, seamless human–robot interaction, and safe autonomous mobility in real-world indoor service environments, as proposed.
> 
> **Operational efficiency and user experience were enhanced** through consistent, personalized, and uninterrupted service delivery enabled by AI-driven learning and decision-making mechanisms.
> 
> **Successful integration of advanced AI and robotics technologies** including natural language processing, computer vision, and machine learning was achieved, resulting in a scalable and cost-effective humanoid service platform.

10\. **<span class="underline">Benefits of the Project:</span>**

1.  Market Potential

<!-- end list -->

1.  **Patient Assistance and Navigation**

> HARP’s multimodal perception, emotion recognition, and multilingual communication capabilities enable it to guide patients and visitors to departments, clinics, and facilities efficiently. This is especially relevant in Pakistan’s linguistically diverse environment, ensuring inclusive and accessible hospital services while reducing staff workload.

2.  **Reception and Information Desk Automation**

> HARP can function as an interactive reception assistant, handling common inquiries, appointment guidance, and real-time information dissemination regarding doctor availability. This helps alleviate congestion at reception counters and enhances overall patient satisfaction.

3.  **Teleoperation and Logistic Support**

> Through teleoperation and autonomous mobility, HARP can assist with routine logistical tasks such as delivering medication, transporting documents, and carrying equipment between wards, reducing physical strain on hospital staff and improving operational flow.

4.  **Infection Control and Contactless Interaction**

> In response to post-pandemic healthcare needs, HARP supports contactless interaction by assisting with symptom screening, information provision, and patient guidance, thereby minimizing unnecessary human contact and reducing infection risks.
> 
> Beyond healthcare, **HARP has strong commercialization potential in banking and corporate service environments**. Initial discussions are underway with **JS Bank** to explore the deployment of humanoid assistants for customer engagement and service automation, highlighting the platform’s adaptability across multiple service industries.

2.  End Users/Organizations which can utilize output.

> Hospitals, Banks, Hotels, University receptions etc

3.  Marketing / Commercialization Strategy

> To commercialize the **Humanoid Assistant Robotic Platform (HARP)**, we are targeting **private and public-sector hospitals** through pilot deployments and strategic partnerships. Demonstrations highlighting HARP’s capabilities in patient assistance, emotion recognition, and multilingual interaction are being used to showcase improvements in operational efficiency and patient experience.
> 
> HARP was showcased at the **ASEAN APTEX exhibition**, where it received strong interest and led to **international research collaborations**, demonstrating its broader market potential. To support scaling and deployment, **research proposals have been submitted under the RASTA/PIDE and NRPU funding schemes**, and **initial discussions are underway with JS Bank** to explore commercialization opportunities in customer-facing service environments.

17\. **<span class="underline">Socio-Economic Impact of the Project (Compulsory).</span>**

> The **Humanoid Assistant Robotic Platform (HARP)** has significant socio-economic impact potential in Pakistan, particularly in the healthcare sector. By automating routine tasks such as patient navigation, data collection, and reception duties, HARP can reduce the burden on overworked hospital staff, improving healthcare accessibility and quality. Its development and deployment will create job opportunities and foster skill development in robotics, AI, and technical support, contributing to local innovation and technological advancement. HARP also offers cost-effective solutions for hospitals, enabling resource optimization and better patient care. Its multilingual capabilities ensure social inclusion for Pakistan's diverse population, while its contactless interaction features enhance public health safety, especially during pandemics. Overall, HARP drives innovation, boosts economic growth, and improves healthcare efficiency, making it a transformative solution for the country's healthcare challenges.

18\. **<span class="underline">Reasons of delay if the project is completed after the approved duration:</span>**

> The project titled *“Humanoid Assistant Robotic Platform (HARP)”* (Project No. **FSP-23-13**) was approved on **17 November 2023** for a duration of **12 months**. Delays in project execution were primarily due to human resource constraints, which led to two **cost-neutral extension requests**, as outlined below.
> 
> **First Extension (up to 11 May 2025):**  
> Although the project was approved in November 2023, the hiring of the Research Assistant was completed on **12 February 2024**. Subsequently, the Research Assistant resigned after serving for **seven months on 12 September 2024**, causing a disruption in project continuity. Due to the early resignation, several planned activities could not be completed within the original project timeline. Consequently, a **cost-neutral first extension** was requested up to **11 May 2025** to allow completion of the remaining tasks in accordance with the approved project scope.
> 
> **Second Extension (up to 30 December 2025):**  
> Following the approval of the first extension, multiple hiring drives were conducted to recruit a replacement Research Assistant. One candidate accepted briefly but declined before proper joining and office order immediately, while two shortlisted candidates declined the offer due to limited remuneration under the approved budget. A subsequent hiring drive also did not result in a suitable appointment owing to the same financial constraints. As a result, minor but essential activities, including system refinement, integration, and iterative testing, remained incomplete. In view of these continued human resource challenges, a **second cost-neutral extension** was requested up to **30 December 2025**, along with permission to engage undergraduate and postgraduate students under the approved HR budget to ensure timely completion of the project.

19\. **<span class="underline">Recommendations on further utilization of results / output indicating commercialization and marketing:</span>**

> While the current HARP prototype has successfully demonstrated core functionalities including perception, interaction, and autonomous navigation, it remains a **research and development prototype** rather than a market-ready product. Further engineering refinement and feature expansion are required to transition HARP into a fully deployable commercial system.
> 
> Future development should focus on enhancing physical manipulation capabilities, such as the integration of articulated arms, to enable object handling and task execution required in real service environments. Additional work is also recommended on industrial-grade hardware design, long-term reliability testing, safety certifications, and user-centric interface refinement.
> 
> From a commercialization perspective, the current prototype serves as a strong **technology demonstrator** and proof-of-concept that can be leveraged to attract industry partners, pilot deployments, and follow-on funding. Targeted collaborations with service providers, banks, hospitals, and public facilities can facilitate phased commercialization through controlled pilots before full-scale market entry.

20**. <span class="underline">Major problems faced during the execution of project, if any:-</span>**

> One of the primary challenges encountered during the execution of the project was **limited financial resources**, which necessitated prioritizing expenditures toward essential hardware, sensors, and computing equipment required for prototype development.
> 
> As a result, comparatively fewer resources were available for hiring research assistants. The limited remuneration offered under these constraints reduced the pool of interested and qualified candidates, which impacted manpower availability and, in turn, project execution timelines.
> 
> Despite these limitations, the project objectives were successfully achieved through optimized resource utilization and focused development efforts.
